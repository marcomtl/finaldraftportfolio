My Favorite TV Sitcoms

Spongebob

Spongebob is a show about the misadventures of a talking sea sponge who works at a fast food restaurant, attends a boating school, and lives in an underwater pineapple.

South Park

South Park is an animated series featuring four foul-mouthed 4th graders, Stan, Kyle, Kenny and Cartman. The show is set in the Colorado town of South Park where weird things keep happening, whether its being abducted by aliens or avoiding Kyle's little brother Ike.

That's So Raven

That's so Raven centers on Raven Baxter (Raven-Symoné), a teenager with hidden psychic abilities. Episodes show Raven experiencing visions of future events while she must also deal with the social and personal issues of her youth.

Robot Chicken

Robot Chicken is an American adult animated stop motion sketch comedy television series.

Martin

Martin is a sassy sitcom centering on a radio-and-television personality named Martin Payne. The series focuses on Martin's romantic relationship with girlfriend Gina Waters, his job changes from a radio personality to a television personality, and the variety of friends Martin hangs out with along the way.

Family Guy

Family guy is set in a wacky Rhode Island town, depicting a dysfunctional family strive to cope with everyday life as they are thrown from one crazy scenario to another. Sick, twisted and politically incorrect, the animated series features the adventures of the Griffin family.

The Cleavland Show

Being a spin-off from family guy, The Cleavland Show is an animated series focuses on the character of Cleveland and his family as he moves from Rhode Island to Virginia.

George Lopez

George Lopez stars the titular comedian George Lopez, who plays a fictionalized version of himself and revolves around his life at work and raising his family at home.

American Dad

American Dad! centers on the absurd circumstances, adventures and domestic life of its title character Stan Smith, his immediate family, and their three housemates.

For this page I decided to focus on my favorite tv sitcoms. These shows brought me back to my childhood and shows that I grew up watching. These shows give me memorabilia and that’s why they all are special to me. Again I recommend all of these shows to anyone on this site who enjoys watching comedy. The only issue is that some of these shows are discontinued and old, while others are still being released until this day.

These shows are typically more family friendly than series. They are also much easier to watch since they are only about 20 to 30 minutes long. I’ve incorporated a mixture of cartoon and reality sitcoms. Shows like Family Guy, Cleveland Show, and American Dad have the same creators; this is similar to how Power and BMF both came from the same producer. If a creator makes one thing I like, chances are I will find another art of theirs that I enjoy. Shows like Martin and George Lopez are sitcoms that are based around a fictional life of the show’s creator. The shows I particularly enjoyed because I was familiar with the lead actors in movies and I found them to be genuinely funny.
